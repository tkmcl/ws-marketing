## 1. Executives in Oil & Gas

### Primary Persona: "Strategic Sarah"
**Role:** VP of Planning & Development, Major E&P Operator
**Demographics:** 48, MBA + Petroleum Engineering background, 25 years in industry
**Company Profile:** Tier 1 operator (Equinor, Shell, BP scale), $15B+ annual capex, global portfolio

**Responsibilities:**
- Portfolio prioritization and capital allocation across business units
- FID recommendations to C-suite and board
- Long-range planning (5-20 year horizons)
- Cross-functional alignment (subsurface, drilling, facilities, finance)

**Key Challenges:**
- Capital discipline pressure from investors (buyback expectations vs. growth)
- Balancing short-term production targets with long-term value creation
- Managing uncertainty (commodity prices, regulatory shifts, energy transition)
- Too many "good" projects competing for limited capital
- Difficulty seeing true trade-offs across diverse portfolio

**Decision Criteria:**
- ROI and NPV, but increasingly also optionality and resilience
- Board-defensible rationale (audit trail, scenario robustness)
- Speed to decision without sacrificing rigor
- Alignment with corporate strategy (emissions targets, basin focus)

**Information Sources:**
- Board presentations and investor reports from peers
- Industry conferences (CERAWeek, ONS, ADIPEC)
- Direct relationships with service company executives
- Internal strategy and planning teams

**AI/Digital Attitude:**
- Cautiously optimistic — has seen hype cycles before
- Wants proven value, not pilots that don't scale
- Skeptical of "black box" recommendations
- Values transparency in how decisions are supported

**Pain Quote:**
> "We have more data than ever, but I still can't get a clear answer on which projects to prioritize. Every team advocates for their own assets."

**WhiteSpace Hook:**
Strategic clarity across the portfolio. See the trade-offs. Defend the decision.

## 2. Engineers in Field Development & Well Planning

### Primary Persona: "Practical Peter"
**Role:** Senior Field Development Engineer, Operator
**Demographics:** 38, MSc Petroleum Engineering, 14 years experience
**Company Profile:** Major or large independent, multiple active FDPs in execution

**Responsibilities:**
- Concept selection and FEED for new developments
- Well count, placement, and sequencing optimization
- Coordinating inputs from subsurface, drilling, facilities, and economics teams
- Managing FDP iterations through gate reviews

**Key Challenges:**
- Endless scenario iterations with manual spreadsheet/model updates
- Subsurface uncertainty driving wide outcome ranges
- Cross-discipline alignment — everyone optimizes their silo
- Time pressure: management wants answers faster than rigorous analysis allows
- "What-if" requests that derail planned workflows

**Tools Currently Used:**
- Petrel, Eclipse, Intersect (subsurface/reservoir)
- Spotfire, Power BI (visualization)
- Excel (still the integration layer for most workflows)
- Internal proprietary planning models

**Decision Criteria:**
- Technical defensibility (peer review, uncertainty quantification)
- Speed of iteration without sacrificing model integrity
- Ability to trace assumptions through to outputs
- Works with existing data and tools (no rip-and-replace)

**AI/Digital Attitude:**
- Pragmatic adopter — will use it if it saves time and is trustworthy
- Wary of tools that oversimplify complex reservoir/drilling interactions
- Wants to understand the "why" behind recommendations
- Hates tools that create more work (data prep, formatting)

**Pain Quote:**
> "I ran 47 scenarios last month. Each one took two days of manual updates. And then management asked for five more."

**WhiteSpace Hook:**
Run more scenarios in less time. See the trade-offs without the spreadsheet hell.

## 3. Digital Transformation Managers

### Primary Persona: "Digital Dana"
**Role:** Head of Digital / Chief Digital Officer, E&P Operator
**Demographics:** 45, Computer Science background with MBA, 8 years in O&G (joined from tech)
**Company Profile:** Major or large independent, active digital transformation program

**Responsibilities:**
- Digital strategy and roadmap for upstream operations
- Vendor evaluation and technology partnerships
- Scaling pilots to enterprise deployment
- Change management and user adoption
- IT/OT convergence and data architecture

**Key Challenges:**
- Pilot purgatory: successful PoCs that never scale
- Competing vendor ecosystems (SLB Delfi, Halliburton DS365, internal platforms)
- Data quality and integration across siloed systems
- Skeptical user base ("we've seen digital initiatives before")
- Proving ROI to justify continued investment

**Decision Criteria:**
- Integration with existing tech stack (OSDU, cloud platforms)
- Time to value (months, not years)
- User adoption metrics (not just technical success)
- Vendor stability and long-term partnership potential
- Total cost of ownership (licensing, integration, support)

**Vendor Evaluation Process:**
- RFI/RFP with IT and business stakeholders
- Pilot with defined success criteria (typically 3-6 months)
- Security and compliance review
- Reference calls with peer operators

**AI/Digital Attitude:**
- True believer in potential, frustrated by execution challenges
- Allergic to "AI-washing" — wants specifics on what the AI actually does
- Values transparency, explainability, and auditability
- Focused on user adoption as the ultimate success metric

**Pain Quote:**
> "I've got 15 pilots running. Three might scale. The rest will die because nobody wants to change how they work."

**WhiteSpace Hook:**
Built for adoption. Integrates with your stack. Value in weeks, not years.

## How to Use These Personas

**For Messaging Development:**
- Write copy as if speaking directly to one persona
- Test whether the message addresses their specific pain, not generic benefits
- Use their language (executives talk ROI and strategy; engineers talk workflows and data)

**For Content Planning:**
- Map content types to personas: thought leadership → executives; technical deep-dives → engineers; case studies → digital leads
- Ensure each piece has a clear primary audience

**For Sales Enablement:**
- Use personas to anticipate objections by role
- Tailor demo flows to persona priorities
- Prepare role-specific proof points and references

**For Competitive Positioning:**
- Test differentiation claims against each persona's decision criteria
- Identify which persona each competitor is strongest with
- Find gaps where WhiteSpace can win
