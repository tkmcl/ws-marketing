# Account Deep Dive: Harbour Energy (HBR.L)

## 1. Strategy & Priorities (2026+)
Harbour has shifted from "Integration" to "High-Grade Hubs." Following the successful exit of the Wintershall Dea Transitional Services Agreement in Sept 2025, 2026 is their first year as a fully integrated global independent operator.

### Strategic Priorities
*   **Asset High-Grading**: Aggressive reallocation of capital toward "International Growth Hubs" (Norway, Mexico, Indonesia) while "Harvesting" legacy UKCS assets to fund growth. The Q2 2026 sale of Indonesian assets (Natuna Sea/Tuna) to Prime Group for $215M is a signal of moving away from fragmented assets to consolidated hubs.
*   **Production Targets**: Aiming to sustain >450 kboepd through 2027.
*   **Capital Allocation Philosophy**: "Prudent Growth." 2026 is marked by heavy reinvestment into high-return hubs (Norway) while maintaining a $4-5B cost reduction target by 2027 (inherited from the Wintershall merger synergies).
*   **Digital & AI Approach**: Harbour is moving toward **Integrated Activity Planning (IAP)** to manage their expanded global portfolio. Their "Annual Integrated Planning" and "Work Program & Budget" (WP&B) processes are currently undergoing digitization to resolve the complexity of managing 50+ partners across diverse tax regimes.
